About Me
I am a third year undergrad at the University of San Francisco studying Computer Science and set to graduate next May 2017. I am currently spending my summer 2016 doing independent learning of web development via online resources such as w3, codeschool, tutorialspoint, and stackoverflow.

I grew up in Salinas, Monterey in California. My two favorite things growing up were Legos and computers (also video games. And Minecraft. But I still love those two *wink*). Coding just feels like an appropriate combination between my two childhood loves. When I first took an Intro to Java Programming class in my junior year of high school, I thought "ya this is pretty cool." But it was the following year when I finally got a sense of true satisfaction from the end result. I discovered the Minecraft mod known as ComputerCraft, which allowed coding INSIDE OF MINECRAFT. I learned to program in the language Lua, and shortly after took up an interest in Minecraft modding, which led to my full interest in coding.

I've had two years of college CS classes. I've met many friends, encountered many

I have a love for coding. While most people find programming to be a daunting endeavor, I enjoy being able to put together an end result and saying "I made this".

Outside of class you're likely to find me immerse in projects of any size or talking with other students at my campus's Computer Science labs located on the 5th floor of the Harney Science building. I especially love helping lesser-experienced students learn new concepts for their assignments and looking for inspiration in the work of upperclassmen.

Beyond my coursework, I enjoy listening to pop music, playing tennis, learning new songs on my piano and guitar, and performing traditional Filipino and Hawaiian dances for my cultural clubs.
