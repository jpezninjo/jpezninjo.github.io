I'm Graduating Next Spring and I'm Totally Wasting My Summer

I hate the internet. Today I was continueing a tutorial on Rails on Udemy and I was thinking about the age of the instructor. This kid had the voice of a 16 year old, and he knew more about one of the top programming languages in demand in the real world right now than I did, me having 0 knowledgeo of Rails. This realization made me wish that I'd done more with my life prior to this summer. It puts me into the perspective that I'm playing catchup right now to learn all the things that I should've been doing. A year ago I had finished my first year of undergraduate computer science and was rewarding my two semesters of successful CS intro classes with a summer load of video games. I'd always thought that I'd be graduation maybe a semester early, and had always gotten the impression from the upperclassmen in my major to save all my worries about internships and real world til my junior and senior year. Fast forward a year to now. I know for certain that I'm graduating next spring, Class of 2017, having only done 3 years of college. This upcoming year is not only my junior year, but my senior year as well compacted into one sure as hell to be one helluva year. It terrifies me to think that I'm gonna be thrown into the real world in a year from now. While its comforting to know that my upcoming year will consist of half the classes/experience/knowledge that USF's computer science program has to offer (although its still a scary thought that I'm taking as many CS classes in these next two semesters as i've done in the last FOUR, and at the upperdivision level to say the least)...while its comforting to know that the real (probably couldve used a better word) skills are yet to be learned, I'll always be thinking that I couldve and shouldve done more to prepare myself. Cheers to everyone else who's graduating a year early, and best of luck to the challenge ahead that is our junior/senior year.

Keeping Up With Those You've Never Actually Met

I love the internet. Today I was pondering what games to buy on Steam as its close to the July 4th cutoff and none of the games being advertised highly seemed to grab my interest. I hopped on Steam and contacted an old friend I'd met on a modded minecraft server way back when I was senior in high school, 2013-2014 (holy crap its 2016). We'd been partners in-game for months playing with the same community (UGLounge, which we both do still try to keep up with). I've hit him up a few times over the years to say whatsup and I love that we have special relationship where we won't have talked for months and I'll be like "any good games on the Steam summer sale" and we'll just dive into a conversation without any mention of what we've been up to since the last time we talked. I always feel warm on the inside when we chat. I've never met him in real life, don't know what he looks like. I just know three things: he's from Denmark. We're about the same age. And we both have similar interest in games. Oh and we're both chill as hell.
